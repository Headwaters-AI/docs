# Faces

Faces are compiled soul documents. They give AI agents programmable personas as nuanced as real humans using fewer tokens.

Source texts — interviews, essays, journals, transcripts, chat histories — are compiled into plaintext psychological primitives (alpha, beta, delta, epsilon components) that define a coherent and complex persona. At inference time, the relevant components are retrieved and injected into the system prompt in a compressed form that conveys far more character per token than raw text.

## Key concepts

- **Face** — A named persona with compiled psychological primitives. Can be a person, character, or synthetic blend of multiple faces.
- **Compile** — The process that transforms uploaded source texts into structured alpha/beta/delta/epsilon components.
- **Psychological primitives** — The minimal formal language our compiler produces. Compact, retrievable, and composable.
- **Synthetic faces** — Boolean combinations of existing faces (`alice | bob`, `expert & mentor`, etc.) composed on the fly without extra training.
